# source guidelines

## goal

Use reputable, recent, category-appropriate sources instead of generic search-result sludge.

## preferred source order

1. trusted category experts with real testing or deep ownership context
2. recent community consensus from Reddit and specialist forums
3. current market signal from Amazon and other active retailers
4. X/Twitter as a secondary recency and sentiment signal

## category examples

- TVs and monitors: RTINGS, HDTVTest, Notebookcheck, current model-year forum threads
- bags and travel gear: PackHacker, Carryology, Reddit owner threads, long-term review videos from credible reviewers
- headphones and audio: RTINGS, SoundGuys, Crinacle-style measurement-driven sources, owner comfort reports
- laptops and peripherals: Notebookcheck, RTINGS where relevant, Wirecutter when current, enthusiast subreddit threads
- kitchen tools: Serious Eats where relevant, trusted long-term owner feedback, retailer durability patterns

## avoid or downweight

- anonymous SEO affiliate farms
- templated "best X" pages with no real testing details
- copied summaries that cite no dates, no methodology, and no tradeoffs
- social posts with obvious sponsorship energy but no ownership detail

## recency reminders

- fast-moving categories: heavily favor the last 3 to 6 months
- medium-moving categories: prefer the last 12 months
- slow-moving categories: older sources can help, but verify the product is still current and available

## synthesis reminders

- explain why a pick won for this user, not why it is popular in general
- always mention a tradeoff
- if evidence is split, surface the split instead of forcing a fake consensus
